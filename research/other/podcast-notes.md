## Cold Outreach Podcast
**Hosts:** Jack Reamer (SalesBread) + Jeremy Chatelaine (QuickMail)
**Format:** Weekly tactical breakdowns of real cold outreach campaigns
**Where:** Available on Spotify, Apple Podcasts
**Why valuable:** Both hosts are active operators — Jack runs LinkedIn outreach campaigns for clients, Jeremy runs the platform that powers many of them. They share data from real campaigns.

### Notable Episodes

**Episode: LinkedIn Outreach Benchmarks 2024**
- 48.14% positive reply ratio for SalesBread campaigns (vs ~10% industry average for cold email)
- Key driver: Ultra-personalised first lines referencing specific prospect activity
- Fibonacci follow-up sequence: respects prospect time without going silent

**Episode: What's Working in Cold Email Right Now**
- AI personalization that doesn't sound robotic: reference specific company news, recent posts, role changes
- Inbox warming timing: 3–4 weeks minimum before high-volume sends
- Subject lines that mimic internal emails consistently outperform clever/creative ones

**Host:** Morgan Williams
**Episodes:** 130+
**Where:** https://morgandwilliams.com / Spotify
**Why valuable:** Best curated interview-format podcast for B2B SaaS cold outreach specifically. Guests are always active practitioners, not speakers.

### Notable Episodes

**Episode 132: Nick Abraham — 30% LinkedIn Accept Rate + 25% Email Reply Rate**

- Quicklines.ai as a tool for automated first-line personalisation

**Episode: Patrick William Joyce — Building Outbound Sales Teams**
- Fractional outbound team model for B2B founders

## Key Cross-Podcast Themes

1. **Offer-market fit beats copywriting** (Nick Abraham, Morgan Williams, Jack Reamer all agree)
2. **Personalization floor is rising** — what was personalised in 2021 is now table stakes
3. **Multi-channel is table stakes** — email alone is insufficient for most B2B SaaS
4. **Deliverability is infrastructure** — treat domains like servers, not email addresses
5. **Volume + targeting > volume alone** — sending more bad emails makes things worse
