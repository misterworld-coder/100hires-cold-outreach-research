## Post 1 — Cold Email Sequence 101

> Cold email sequence 101: (must-know ahead of 2024)
> Every email in the sequence needs to:
> - Be well-targeted
> - Be 2-3 sentences max
> - Have a no-brainer offer
> - Be personalized and relevant
> - Have a soft CTA offering sales asset
>
> Each sequence should be 4 steps (1 cold, 3 follow-up):
> Email 1 - Offer + Value
> Email 2 - Follow-up + Value/Sales Asset
> Email 3 - Follow-up + Value/Sales Asset
> Email 4 - Break-up
>
> It'd be pretty hard not to see results with:
> - The right cold emails
> - The right sequence settings
> - The right deliverability hygiene

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham (October 2023)
**Key insight:** Short sequences (4 steps) beat long drip campaigns. Each email needs its own value hook — not just a follow-up to the last one.

## Post 2 — The Deliverability Anti-Checklist

> How to guarantee your emails land in the spam folder:
> 6. Don't use spintax.
> 5. Don't use conversational copy.
> 4. Don't clean + format company names, titles, and lead names.
> 3. Don't remove all people from campaign who don't want to be emailed, and add them to your DNC.
> 2. Don't spread inboxes across multiple organizational panels, so they don't share the same subnet IP.
> 1. Don't create more variance in campaign setup (warm-up randomization, sending time variation, etc).
>
> If you want to land in the primary inbox, do the opposite of all of these things.

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham (2023)
**Key insight:** Deliverability is infrastructure, not luck. IP subnet diversity and warm-up randomization are often overlooked by beginners.

## Post 3 — Offer-Market Fit Over Everything

> We booked 57 meeting-ready leads in the first 30 days working with a client.
> (without a crazy lead generation strategy)
>
> This campaign didn't have:
> - A+ email copywriting
> - In-depth personalization
> - A crazy lead scraping method
>
> But the client had the one thing most businesses that fail with cold email are missing:
> **Extreme offer-market fit.**
>
> They offered something incredibly valuable, to an extremely specific clientele.
> We pulled lists of that clientele, and stuck to the cold email principles.

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham (2023)
**Key insight:** Offer-market fit beats copywriting skill. A mediocre email to the right ICP outperforms a great email to a vague audience.

## Post 4 — Volume Is the Answer

> Your cold email problems are simple.
> If:
> - You have a good offer
> - You have good deliverability
> - You follow cold email fundamentals
>
> You'd see better results if you sent more volume.
>
> And the truth is, everyone can do it:
> - Buy more domains
> - Scrape + clean more leads
> - Personalize more messages
>
> You don't have a cold email problem. You have a volume one.

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham (2023)
**Key insight:** Most founders under-invest in volume infrastructure. Domain scaling and lead list depth are operational moats.

## Post 5 — Bad Pitch Teardown: "Are you taking on more clients?"

> The worst cold email pitch of all time:
> "Are you taking on more clients?"
>
> It just sounds so scammy. Any client worth having wouldn't respond.
>
> Instead, say:
> "If I could {generate X result} {risk minimizer} would you be interested in speaking?"
>
> For example:
> "If I could generate meeting-ready leads on a performance basis, would you be interested in speaking?"

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-abraham (October 2023)
**Key insight:** Bait-and-switch openers erode trust. Risk-reversed value propositions convert better than curiosity gaps.
