## Post 1 — Target Your Prospect's "Enemy"

> After 15 years and over $15 million generated doing outbound sales, I've learned that the key is relentlessly targeting your prospect's "enemy".
>
> Their inbox and calendar might be filled with noise, but you need to cut through and grab their attention.
>
> Forget leading with how you can solve their problems — they aren't even aware they have a problem!
>
> Instead, pick on the stuff that annoys them daily, the tedious tasks they dread. Really pick at that sore spot to snap them to attention.
>
> Then you can show them how you eliminate their "enemy" and make their work lives easier.
>
> So don't just pitch your product. Pick a fight with your prospect's status quo, and show them how much better life can be.

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/belbatrawy (2024)
**Key insight:** Lead with friction/pain the prospect already feels — not with your solution. "Enemy" framing creates emotional resonance before the pitch.

## Post 2 — Cold Call Script: The Mic Drop Method

Live cold call script built for Grain (conversation recording tool competing against Gong):

> **Permission opener:**
> "Hey {First Name}, this is Belal from Grain. I'll be upfront, we haven't spoken before. Got 30 seconds for me before you throw your phone out the window?"
>
> **Problem:**
> "A lot of sales leaders are familiar with call recording tools like Gong, Chorus, and Clari. The problem is these tools can be pricey, with some complexity to configure."
>
> [Transition to differentiated value proposition]

**Source:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/belbatrawy (2024)
**Key insight:** Upfront permission + self-aware humor disarms gatekeeping. Naming competitors directly frames your offer without bashing.
